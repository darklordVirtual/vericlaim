As Table 1 shows, depending on different problems, a thought could be a couple of words (Crosswords), a line of equation (Game of 24), or a whole paragraph of writing plan (Creative Writing). In general, a thought should be “small” enough so that LMs can generate promising and diverse samples (e.g. generating a whole book is usually too “big” to be coherent), yet “big” enough so that LMs can evaluate its prospect toward problem solving (e.g. generating one token is usually too “small” to evaluate). 2.
