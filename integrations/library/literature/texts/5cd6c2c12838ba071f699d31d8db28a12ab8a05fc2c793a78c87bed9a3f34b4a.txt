We show how to compute exact attention with fewer HBM reads/writes and without storing large intermediate matrices for the backward pass. This yields an attention algorithm that is both memory efficient and faster
in wall-clock time. We analyze its IO complexity, showing that our method requires
much fewer HBM accesses compared to standard attention. We further show that FlashAttention can serve as a useful primitive by extending it to handle block-sparse attention. We focus here on the forward pass for ease of exposition; Appendix B contains details
for the backward.
