Figure 7: Speedup over standard PyTorch attention at different sequence lengths, on RTX 3090. RTX 3090

Figure 7 shows speedup on an RTX 3090 GPU. Here, we use batch size 12 with 12 attention heads. We observe slightly higher speedups on the RTX 3090 (between 2.5-4.5), since the memory bandwidth on an RTX 3090 is lower than on an A100 (roughly 900 GB/s vs. 1.5 TB/s). Figure 8: Speedup over standard PyTorch attention at different sequence lengths, on T4. Top: Combined forward pass + backward pass. Bottom: Forward pass only. T4

Figure 8 shows speedup on a T4 GPU. T4 SRAM is smaller than A100, so we need to make the block sizes smaller in FlashAttention. As a result, we observe less speedup on T4, which matches the IO complexity analysis in Section 3.2. T4 GPUs are commonly used for inference, so we also report speedup on the forward pass only.
