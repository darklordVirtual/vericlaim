Achieving optimal LLM routing—maximizing quality within a cost constraint or minimizing cost for a target quality—is challenging. An ideal LLM router must (1) optimize response quality while invoking a single LLM per query, minimizing cost and latency as compared to multi-LLM approaches; (2) generalize to out-of-domain queries without needing separate routers for different domains; and (3) work across a broad range of LLMs without retraining, ensuring flexibility as the LLM landscape evolves. In this work, we introduce a principled framework for learning LLM routers from preference data.
