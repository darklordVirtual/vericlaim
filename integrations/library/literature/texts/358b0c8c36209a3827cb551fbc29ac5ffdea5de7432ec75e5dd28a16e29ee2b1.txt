Relatedly, the judge verdicts evaluated here are model-generated for this study; in deployment, judge latency, cost, and refusal behavior on adversarial inputs would all need to be measured against live traffic rather than assumed from the corpus. The online loop is a replay simulation, not a live or production system. The end-to-end self-improving result (Figure 2; Table 5) is a Monte-Carlo replay over 30 streams of 1,500 actions each. Its dynamics are driven by measured verdicts — a cold (no-RAG) and a warm (corroboration- guarded) measurement, gated by the real memory-admission and rule-distillation logic — rather than by re-invoking the judge live on every streamed action. This is the right design for a controlled, reproducible study of the loop’s trajectory (judge-call rate falling 50%44%, judge-domain accuracy rising 71%80%, and 0 benign hard-blocks across 45,000 actions), but it is not a live re-judging system and it is not the production src/ code path.
