The honest scope of these results is a replay study over curated corpora, with hand-set gate thresholds and a single, costly memory guard (§6). The contribution is not a shipped product but a characterization and a design: that the rules-versus-judge question is settled by threat type rather than by effort, that the judge is what makes agent-action trust generalize beyond the rules’ distribution, and that a guarded, self-improving dual-store can get cheaper on the lexical and smarter on the semantic over time without ever hard-blocking a benign action. The clearest paths forward follow directly from the limitations: a real-traffic evaluation, an auto-calibrated confidence gate, additional memory-admission guards, and a productionized online loop.
