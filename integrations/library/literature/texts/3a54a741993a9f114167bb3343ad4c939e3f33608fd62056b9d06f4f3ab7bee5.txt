To evaluate long-term credit assignment capabilities of our model, we consider a variant of the Key-to-Door environment proposed in Mesnard et al. [12]. This is a grid-based environment with a sequence of three phases: (1) in the first phase, the agent is placed in a room with a key; (2) then, the agent is placed in an empty room; (3) and finally, the agent is placed in a room with a door. The agent receives a binary reward when reaching the door in the third phase, but only if it picked up the key in the first phase. This problem is difficult for credit assignment because credit must be propagated from the beginning to the end of the episode, skipping over actions taken in the middle. We train on datasets of trajectories generated by applying random actions and report success rates in Table 6. Furthermore, for the Key-to-Door environment we use the entire episode length as the context, rather than having a fixed content window as in the other environments.
