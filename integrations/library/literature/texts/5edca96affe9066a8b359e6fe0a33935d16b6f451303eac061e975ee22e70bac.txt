Overall, measures of train and test cross entropy are similar across dense and sparse models and across GPT and BIG-G models (Figure 2a). BIG-G sparse models perform better on BIG-bench tasks than BIG-G dense models, achieving a roughly twofold improvement in inference cost (determined by FLOP-matched parameter count) for the same model performance (Figure 2b). Sparse models have far more parameters, however, their total computation is smaller since only a small fraction of the network is activated for each input. The sparse models are modeled closely on the dense BIG-G models by replacing 1-4 feed-forward layers and the FLOP-matched calculation is explained in Appendix A. However, in a new finding, the BIG-G sparse models show dramatic improvements in the calibration of their predictions. They achieve about a tenfold improvement in the FLOP-matched parameter count needed to reach a given calibration score (Figure 5).
