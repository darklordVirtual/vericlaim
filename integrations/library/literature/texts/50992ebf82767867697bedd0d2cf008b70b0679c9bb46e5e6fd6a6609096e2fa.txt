Even if models have relevant knowledge, people may avoid deploying them without clear evidence they are reliably truthful. 3. Malicious misuse. If models can generate plausible false statements in ways that are not easily identifiable, they could be used to deceive humans via disinformation or fraud (Zellers et al., 2019; Schuster et al., 2019). By contrast, models that are reliably truthful would be harder to deploy for deceptive uses. Figure 1: TruthfulQA questions with answers from GPT-3-175B with default prompt.
