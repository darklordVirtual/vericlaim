To achieve a more accurate baseline of human performance on BIG-bench tasks where possible, we relied on a team of expert evaluators who continuously performed evaluation of tasks within the benchmark over the past year. Fig. App.8 visualizes when tasks were evaluated over time. \begin{overpic}[width=346.89731pt]{figures/expert_evaluation.pdf}

Figure App.8:
Timeline of expert evaluation over the course of BIG-bench. Early evaluation was performed over restricted sets of the benchmark. Later testing shifted to more uniformly subsampled evaluation across the full benchmark. Because of the wide diversity of tasks, raters were not always equally calibrated. This made it challenging to develop strong, general measures of human performance for many of the tasks. See Fig. App.9 for a representative comparison of human performance on tasks of differing difficulty. Mean performance across evaluators was typically statistically stable, but often significantly worse than the best evaluator for a task.
