Human Evaluation

Based on the observation that STaR may improve reasoning quality for problems even when they were initially answered correctly via few-shot prompting, we performed a preliminary qualitative analysis. We randomly selected 50 rationales generated from few-shot CoT and STaR-generated rationales on questions which they both answered correctly, as well as human-generated rationales for these problems from [3]. We then presented a random subset of 10 questions and rationales to each of 20 crowdworkers on Prolific [31] with the rationales in a randomized order, asking them to rank the rationales based on which they felt best justified the answer. The participants were 30% more likely to rank the STaR-generated rationales higher than the few-shot rationales ().
