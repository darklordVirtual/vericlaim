It is deliberately adversarial in composition (52% semantic/attack-heavy), which makes it a conservative setting for any amortization claim (§5.4, §6). The external corpus spans eight threat categories that we use throughout: shell_persistence, fs_config, network_egress, cloud_iam_secrets, cicd_iac, supply_chain_pkg, observability_benign_hard, and data_db. Each category mixes benign actions with their malicious surface-twins (e.g. a telemetry curl against an allow-listed endpoint vs. an exfiltration curl to the same endpoint; a debugging kubectl get secret vs. credential theft), which is what makes the lexical/semantic distinction operational rather than cosmetic. Provenance and relationship to v1’s numbers. The internal 300 and independent 630 are v1’s released benchmarks [Yang 2026, §5]: the 300 spans six categories (50 each; 133 allow / 38 warn / 129 block; review is reserved for ambiguity and is never a ground-truth label), and the 630 is a five-batch adversarial suite (a 30-scenario obfuscation probe, two 100-scenario real-world batches, two 200-scenario expansions; 188 / 98 / 344).
