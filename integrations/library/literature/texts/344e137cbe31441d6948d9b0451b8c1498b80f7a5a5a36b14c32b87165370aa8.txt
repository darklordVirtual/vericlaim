Game of 24 is a mathematical reasoning challenge, where the goal is to use 4 numbers and basic arithmetic operations (+-*/) to obtain 24. For example, given input “4 9 10 13”, a solution output could be “(10 - 4) * (13 - 9) = 24”. Figure 2: ToT in a game of 24. The LM is prompted for (a) thought generation and (b) valuation. We scrape data from 4nums.com, which has 1,362 games that are sorted from easy to hard by human solving time, and use a subset of relatively hard games indexed 901-1,000 for testing. For each task, we consider the output as success if it is a valid equation that equals 24 and uses the input numbers each exactly once. We report the success rate across 100 games as the metric. Baselines. We use a standard input-output (IO) prompt with 5 in-context examples.
