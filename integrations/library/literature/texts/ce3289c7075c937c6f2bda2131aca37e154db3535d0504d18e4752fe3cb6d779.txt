Our claims are three structural ones that the literature below does not make: (1) a decomposition of agent- action threats into a lexical / fixed-signature class and a semantic / intent-dependent class, together with a negative result that deterministic rules cannot cross the boundary regardless of how many rules are added; (2) a cross-distribution cross-provider characterization showing the judge — not the rules — is the component that generalizes; and (3) a self-improving dual-store in which the judge acts as a teacher that distills a fast deterministic rule "student" on the lexical class and curates a guarded retrieval memory on the semantic class, under a never-hard-block-a-benign-action safety invariant.
