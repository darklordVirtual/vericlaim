Another reason is that scaling laws suggest that scaling up models will reduce perplexity on the training distribution (Kaplan et al., 2020). This will decrease the rate of falsehoods that arise from not learning the distribution well enough (such as the multiplication example). Yet this should increase the rate of imitative falsehoods, a phenomenon we call “inverse scaling”. Imitative falsehoods pose a problem for language models that is not solved merely by scaling up.
