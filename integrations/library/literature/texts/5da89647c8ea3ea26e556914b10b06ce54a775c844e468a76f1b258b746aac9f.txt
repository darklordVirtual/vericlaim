In this section, we seek to gain insight into whether Decision Transformer can be thought of as performing imitation learning on a subset of the data with a certain return. To investigate this, we propose a new method, Percentile Behavior Cloning (%BC), where we run behavior cloning on only the top of timesteps in the dataset, ordered by episode returns. The percentile interpolates between standard BC () that trains on the entire dataset and only cloning the best observed trajectory (), trading off between better generalization by training on more data with training a specialized model that focuses on a desirable subset of the data. Note that the only way to choose the optimal subset for cloning is to evaluate using rollouts from the environment, so %BC is not a realistic approach; rather, it serves to provide insight into the behavior of Decision Transformer. When data is plentiful – as in the D4RL regime – we find %BC can match or beat other offline RL methods.
