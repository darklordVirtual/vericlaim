A Russian Defense Ministry Tu-154 plane with 92 people on board crashed into the Black Sea on Sunday morning shortly after taking off from the southern Russian city of Sochi. (Yuri Kochetkov/EPA)
According to the ministry, the plane was found at a depth of more than 1,000 feet and the recorder was damaged. ”The defense ministry has decided to hand over the recorder to the Interstate Aviation Committee, which is the main investigative body in the air crash,” the statement said. The search for the plane’s black boxes is being hampered by strong winds and rough seas. Investigators have launched a criminal probe into the crash, which officials said was likely caused by a technical fault. Russian President Vladimir Putin declared Wednesday a day of mourning for the victims.
