Training data extraction attacks are often seen as theoretical
or academic and are thus unlikely to be exploitable in practice [71]. This is justified by the prevailing intuition that privacy leakage is
correlated with overfitting [72],
and because state-of-the-art LMs are trained on large (near terabyte-sized [7]) datasets for a few epochs, they tend to not overfit [53]. Our paper demonstrates that training data extraction attacks
are practical. To accomplish this, we first precisely define what we mean by
“memorization”. We then state our threat model and our attack objectives. Finally, we discuss the ethical considerations behind these attacks and explain why they are likely to be a serious threat in the future.
