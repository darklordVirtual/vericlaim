The dynamics loss trains the sequence model to predict the next representation by minimizing the KL divergence between the predictor and the next stochastic representation . The representation loss, in turn, trains the representations to become more predictable allowing us to use a factorized dynamics predictor for fast sampling during imagination training. The two losses differ in the stop-gradient operator and their loss scale. To avoid a degenerate solution where the dynamics are trivial to predict but fail to contain enough information about the inputs, we employ free bits26 by clipping the dynamics and representation losses below the value of 1 nat 1.44 bits.
