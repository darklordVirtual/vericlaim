In this work, we explore evaluation via few lookahead simulations (e.g. quickly confirm that 5, 5, 14 can reach 24 via 5 + 5 + 14, or “hot_l” can mean “inn” via filling “e” in “_”) plus commonsense (e.g. 1 2 3 are too small to reach 24, or no word can start with “tzxc”). While the former might promote “good” states, the latter could help eliminate “bad” states. Such valuations do not need to be perfect, and only need to be approximately helpful for decision making. Vote across states: , where a “good” state is voted out based on deliberately comparing different states in in a vote prompt. When problem success is harder to directly value (e.g. passage coherency), it is natural to to instead compare different partial solutions and vote for the most promising one. This is similar in spirit to a “step-wise” self-consistency strategy, i.e. cast “which state to explore” as a multi-choice QA, and use LM samples to vote for it.
