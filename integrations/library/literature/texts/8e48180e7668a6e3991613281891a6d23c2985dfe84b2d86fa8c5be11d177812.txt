Figure 3 shows the full pipeline and the learning loop. What v2 inherits, and what it adds. The deterministic core is v1’s [Yang 2026]: a ShellNormalizer (nine pure-text deobfuscation strategies — v1’s single highest-value component, which alone halves the obfuscated-attack miss rate in ablation), a 42-pattern ActionAnalyzer, a 170-rule PolicyEngine that already covers cloud IAM (AWS/GCP/Azure), containers, Kubernetes, database privilege, credential files, and anti-forensics, a SafeFix remediation engine, a RiskChain multi-step detector, a cache-aware LLM-Judge, and a TrustReporter. v2 keeps this entire core as its rule floor and makes two additions that turn a static interceptor into a self-evolving system: (i) it promotes the confidence-gated judge from an opt-in fallback to the evaluated core (§4.3), and (ii) it couples floor and judge through a self-improving dual-store — judge-to-rule distillation on the lexical side, a guarded retrieval memory on the semantic side — so the system learns from its own decisions (§4.5).
