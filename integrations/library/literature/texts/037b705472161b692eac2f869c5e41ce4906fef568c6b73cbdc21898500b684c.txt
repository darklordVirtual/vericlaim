Instead of emphasizing the representation of the question, we focus on the model output; in particular, we focus on the model’s ability to reason through a problem before coming to a conclusion. Rationales

One of the initial works on the impact of rationales on language model performance was rajani2019explain [3], showing that training a language model on a dataset with explicit rationales preceding the answer could improve a model’s ability to generate the final answer. However, this required many thousands of training examples to be manually annotated with human reasoning. Recently, nye2021show [5] demonstrated that step-by-step “scratchpads” can improve fine-tuned LLM performance and generalization on tasks such as arithmetic, polynomial evaluation, and program evaluation. Similarly, wei2022chain [6] used a single few-shot “chain-of-thought” reasoning prompt in order to improve model performance on a collection of tasks, without fine-tuning.
