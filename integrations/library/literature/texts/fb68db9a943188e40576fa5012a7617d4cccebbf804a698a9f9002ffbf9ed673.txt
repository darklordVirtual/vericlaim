The AI community’s distributed expertise in the process of AI development and the feasibility of different technical scenarios could thus be brought to bear to limit AI "arms racing" and prevent a race to the bottom with respect to the safety, security, and human-accountability of deployed military AI systems [166]. The feasibility of verifying, interpreting, and testing potential AI systems designed for various purposes, as well as the feasibility of using different points of control for governance of the supply chain (e.g., the computing and non-computing hardware associated with autonomous weapons vs. the underlying software), are all issues to which AI expertise is relevant. Of the various inputs into AI development (including hardware, software, data, and human effort), it’s worth noting that hardware is uniquely governable, at least in principle.
