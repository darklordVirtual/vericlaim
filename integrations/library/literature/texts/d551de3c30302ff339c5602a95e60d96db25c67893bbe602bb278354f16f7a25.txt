Our training tasks are from two sources: (1) a dataset of prompts written by our labelers and (2) a dataset of prompts submitted to early InstructGPT models on our API (see Table 6). These prompts are very diverse and include generation, question answering, dialog, summarization, extractions, and other natural language tasks (see Table 2). Our dataset is over 96% English, however in Section 4.3 we also probe our model’s ability to respond to instructions in other languages and complete coding tasks. For each natural language prompt, the task is most often specified directly through a natural language instruction (e.g. “Write a story about a wise frog”), but could also be indirectly through either few-shot examples (e.g. giving two examples of frog stories, and prompting the model to generate a new one) or implicit continuation (e.g. providing the start of a story about a frog). In each case, we ask our labelers to do their best to infer the intent of the user who wrote the prompt, and ask them to skip inputs where the task is very unclear.
