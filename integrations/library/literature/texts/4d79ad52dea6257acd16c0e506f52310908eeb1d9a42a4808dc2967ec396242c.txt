Performance of the evaluated models on low-resource languages is generally low. The swahili_english_proverbs task is a multiple-choice task that tests a model’s ability to find an appropriate English correspondence for a proverb from Swahili, a Bantu language spoken by around 100 million people in East Africa and written with a Latin script. Figure 15a displays a trend of increasing performance with model scale, with the best model achieving 43% accuracy on when given four choices. However, it is not clear whether this performance indicates general understanding of Swahili or instead memorization of proverbs listed on the internet. The kannada task poses multiple-choice riddles with four possible answers in Kannada, a Dravidian language spoken by around 40 million people in India and written using a non-Latin script. As seen in Figure 15b, none of the evaluated models significantly exceeds random chance performance. The BIG-bench task covering the most low-resource languages is language_identification, requiring models to identify which language a given sentence is written in. Questions are posed as multiple choice with ten possible answers.
