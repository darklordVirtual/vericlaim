We further investigate whether increasing the coefficient of KL reward ( in Equation 2) is sufficient to fix the regressions on public NLP datasets, using the 1.3B model. We set the pretraining loss coefficient to 0 and sweep a range of KL reward coefficient’s uniformly in log linear space. The results are shown in Figure 34. The pretrained GPT model is used as the KL reward model, in these experiments. We find that even by increasing the KL reward coefficient to 2.0, which is 100 times of the default value, the regressions still cannot be fixed. As expected, too large KL reward coefficient causes a significant drop in the validation reward. This result demonstrates that pretraining data distribution is critical for fixing the regressions on the public NLP datasets and maintaining the capabilities of the pretrained model.
