Given a set of samples from the model, the problem of training data extraction reduces to one of membership
inference: predict whether each sample was present in the training data [65]. In their most basic form, past membership inference attacks rely on the observation that models tend to assign higher confidence to examples that are present in the training data [46]. Therefore, a potentially high-precision membership inference classifier is to simply choose examples that are assigned the highest likelihood by the model.
