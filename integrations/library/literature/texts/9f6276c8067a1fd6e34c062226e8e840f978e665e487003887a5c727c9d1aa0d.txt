Securities law is premised on the theory that mandated disclosure will improve corporate behavior, and SEC’s EDGAR database allows users to search corporate filings, find out who audited which company, and access audit reports. Recent changes even provide the name of the audit partner. Mandatory disclosure requirements, however, are not costless. One study found that publicly traded companies spend an average of $2.2 million to comply with the Sarbanes-Oxley Act’s financial reporting requirements (Krishnan et al., 2008). Another analysis of restaurant grading across ten jurisdictions found that the practice—meant to protect consumers and improve food sanitation via targeted disclosures—suffered from several serious deficiencies, including grade inflation and grading inconsistencies. The implementation of a restaurant grading scheme shifted valuable inspection resources away from more significant safety hazards (Ho, 2012).
