The construction of BIG-bench was organized as an open collaboration on GitHub. Several other benchmarks have been organized through similar community efforts, and this approach has increasingly been adopted to source diverse ideas and contributions. To address the lack of geographic diversity in NLP, Masakhane used participatory research to construct a machine-translation benchmark for over 30 low-resource languages (Nekoto et al., 2020). EleutherAI’s Language Model Evaluation Harness (Gao et al., 2022) is another open-source collaborative benchmarking codebase, though unlike BIG-bench it does not introduce new tasks; it instead organizes existing tasks and defines a unified API for evaluating models on them. The GEM benchmark is a living benchmark for natural language generation, evaluation, and metrics
(Gehrmann et al., 2021), constructed by a broad collaboration of more than 50 contributors. GEM provides an environment in which models can
be tested on a wide set of tasks and with different evaluation strategies that can evolve alongside the models.
