Third-party reporting has a similar effect on small businesses (Adhikari et al., 2021). Still, consumer complaints have proven to be a valuable signal when harms can be difficult to observe. One analysis of investigated nursing home complaints, for example, found that the complaints predicted performance at subsequent inspections (Stevenson, 2006). Another research study found that the Vaccine Adverse Event Reporting System (VAERS) database is predictive of true safety concerns with newly released vaccines (Geier and Geier, 2004). Complaint-initiated audits, however, also raise challenges. Voluntary reporting can be unreliable and create reporting bias (Altenburger and Ho, 2019). Residents in low-income and minority communities, for instance, are less likely to complain about street conditions or “nuisance” issues in a 311 system (Kontokosta and Hong, 2021). Similar biases have been found in recent consumer complaints to the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB) (Porter, 2012)—although early CFPB complaints, if anything, appeared to be higher from minority neighborhoods (Ayres et al., 2013).
