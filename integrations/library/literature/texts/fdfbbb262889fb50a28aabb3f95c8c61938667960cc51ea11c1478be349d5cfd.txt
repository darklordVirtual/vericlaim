Other examples of incident reporting systems abound, including the FDA’s medical device incident database (Food and Administration, 2022), the Federal Aviation Administration’s Aviation Safety Reporting System (Administration, 2022), NIST’s National Vulnerabilities Database (NIST, 2022), and the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau’s complaint system (Pluta, 2013). These third-party reporting systems can help focus auditing resources on issues that matter most (Tatonetti et al., 2012). Third-party reporting is most successful when third parties possess high quality information. When an individual’s income is reported to the IRS by third parties like banks and employers, for example, that individual is far more likely to be tax compliant than if they self-reported their income (Kleven et al., 2011; Johnson and Rose, 2019). Third-party reporting has a similar effect on small businesses (Adhikari et al., 2021).
