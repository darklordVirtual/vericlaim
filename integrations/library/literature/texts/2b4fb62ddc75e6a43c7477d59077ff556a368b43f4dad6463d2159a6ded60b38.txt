We first unify approaches for using test-time computation and then analyze some representative methods. First, we view the use of additional test-time compute through the lens of modifying the model’s predicted distribution adaptively at test-time, conditioned on a given prompt. Ideally, test-time compute should modify the distribution so as to generate better outputs than naïvely sampling from the LLM itself would. In general, there are two knobs to induce modifications to an LLM’s distribution: (1) at the input level: by augmenting the given prompt with an additional set of tokens that the LLM conditions on to obtain the modified distribution, or (2) at the output level: by sampling multiple candidates from the standard LM and performing surgery on these candidates. In other words, we could either modify the proposal distribution induced by the LLM itself such that it is an improvement over naïvely conditioning on the prompt or we could use some post-hoc verifiers or scorers to perform output modifications.
