The two questions are related. Particularly competitive contexts (e.g., autonomous vehicles) might be simultaneously very appealing and challenging: multiple parties stand to gain from pooling of insights, but collaborative red teaming in such contexts is also challenging because of intellectual property and security concerns. As an alternative to or supplement to explicitly collaborative red teaming, organizations building AI technologies should establish shared resources and outlets for sharing relevant non-proprietary information. The subsection on sharing of AI incidents also discusses some potential innovations that could alleviate concerns around sharing proprietary information.
