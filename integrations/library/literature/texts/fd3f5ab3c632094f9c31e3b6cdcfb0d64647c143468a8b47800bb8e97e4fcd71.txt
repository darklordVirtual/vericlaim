Second, we survey audit systems across domains – e.g., financial, environmental, and health regulation – and show that the institutional design of such audits are far from monolithic. Finally, we survey the evidence base around these design components and spell out the implications for algorithmic auditing. We conclude that the turn toward audits alone is unlikely to achieve actual algorithmic accountability, and sustained focus on institutional design will be required for meaningful third party involvement. auditing, policy, algorithms, accountability, society
