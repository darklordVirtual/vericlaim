This is not a hypothesis about under-investment in rule-writing; it is a claim about a structural ceiling. We establish it with a determined steelman. Starting from an honest deterministic rule pack, we hand-authored an additional cloud/CI/Kubernetes rule pack on development data and tested it on a held-out, realistic corpus. The added rules lift overall held-out accuracy from 48% to 56% — exactly the kind of gain one expects when more lexical signatures are added — yet they move the purely semantic categories by 0 percentage points: data-and-database threats stay at 2929, observability/benign-hard cases at 5959, and supply-chain threats at 5050 (Table 3). A separate, maximally token-tuned "cheat" rule set, optimized directly against the data, also lands at 47.6% overall: in-distribution tuning does not transfer. Rules close on the lexical categories and plateau, untouched, on the semantic ones.
