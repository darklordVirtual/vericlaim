Bias can potentially be steered through appropriately chosen prompting. It has been widely observed that the character of model output can be steered through choice of prompt. For example, prompts may aim to elicit jokes, particular literary styles, or factual responses (Branwen, 2020), to regurgitate memorized information (Carlini et al., 2021), or to encourage models to be better assistants (Askell et al., 2021). Furthermore, model performance often improves in few-shot evaluation relative to zero-shot performance, indicating that models can learn from in-context examples (Prabhumoye et al., 2021). Appropriately chosen prompting, or in-context examples, can alter output bias.
