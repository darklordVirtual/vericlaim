The adjustment involved computing conformal scores, which grow when the model is uncertain, but are not valid prediction intervals on their own. In our case, the conformal score was one minus the softmax output of the true class, but in general, the score can be any function of and . We then took to be roughly the quantile of the scores. In this case, the quantile had a simple interpretation—when setting , at least of ground truth softmax outputs are guaranteed to be above the level (we prove this rigorously in Appendix LABEL:app:coverage-proof).
