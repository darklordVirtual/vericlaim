(2020) studied calibration on generative language models (T5, BART, and GPT-2) and found that their predictive probabilities on question-answering tasks are not well calibrated. In order to probe calibration, we measured model calibration on
JSON multiple-choice
tasks across model scales. Since a model’s multiple-choice selection is based on the conditional log likelihood scores of the target choices, normalized (top-1) probability can be treated as model confidence for tasks where there is one correct choice. Using this confidence score, we compute Brier score (Brier, 1950) and expected calibration error (ECE, Naeini et al., 2015) for each model size. While Brier score is a proper scoring rule for measuring the accuracy of predicted probabilities, ECE has been widely used to measure calibration due to its intuitive nature. See Ovadia et al. (2019) for discussion of properties and drawbacks of various calibration metrics.
