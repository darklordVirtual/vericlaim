To extract a model , without knowledge of the model class, a simple strategy is to extract a multilayer perceptron with a large enough hidden layer. Indeed, feed-forward networks with a single hidden layer can, in principle, closely approximate any continuous function over a bounded subset of [28, 20]. However, this strategy intuitively does not appear to be optimal. Even if we know that we can find a multilayer perceptron that closely matches , might have a far more complex representation (more parameters) than . Thus, tailoring the extraction to the ‘simpler’ model class of the target appears more efficient. In learning theory, the problem of finding a succinct representation of some target model is known as Occam Learning [13]. Our experiments indicate that such generic improper extraction indeed appears sub-optimal, in the context of equation-solving attacks.
