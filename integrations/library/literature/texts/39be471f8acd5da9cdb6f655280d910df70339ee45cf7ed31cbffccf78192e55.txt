Conformal outlier detection can also be seen as a hypothesis testing problem; points that are rejected as outliers have a p-value less than for the null hypothesis of exchangeability with the calibration data. This interpretation is closely related to the classical permutation test [fisher1936design, pitman1937significance]. See [vovk2003testing, guan2019prediction, bates2021multiple]
for more on this interpretation and other statistical properties of conformal outlier detection.
