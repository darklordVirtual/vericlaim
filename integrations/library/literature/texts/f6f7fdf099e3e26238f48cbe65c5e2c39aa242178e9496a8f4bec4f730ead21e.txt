How might we craft an interactive artificial society that reflects believable human behavior? From sandbox games such as The Sims to applications such as cognitive models (Card et al., 1983) and virtual environments (Laird and VanLent, 2001; Bates, 1994), for over four decades, researchers and practitioners have envisioned computational agents that can serve as believable proxies of human behavior. In these visions, computationally-powered agents act consistently with their past experiences and react believably to their environments.
