Protecting proprietary information is not a proper response, as all audit systems provide some form of privileged access to auditors, and disclosure does not need to be direct nor absolute. The National Institute of Standards and Technology, for instance, protects models by having companies run models via a custom Application Program Interface (API) for the Face Recognition Vendor Test (FRVT). Such mediated access, subject to auditor vetting (perhaps by an audit oversight board) and consistent with audit scope, will be critical to enabling third party auditing of AI systems.
