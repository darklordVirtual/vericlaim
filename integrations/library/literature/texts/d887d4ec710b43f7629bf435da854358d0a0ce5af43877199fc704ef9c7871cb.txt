Mean performance across evaluators was typically statistically stable, but often significantly worse than the best evaluator for a task. Instead of a more complicated measure, we erred on the side of simplicity, and chose to also report the best average score across the set of evaluators---a set that included one of the core authors. For the sake of expediency, tasks were evaluated with implicit time controls and task subsampling (i.e., over evaluation sessions of a fixed length running between 30 minutes and two hours). This imposition almost certainly reduced the ceiling of performance for the evaluators. We strongly suspect that for every task in BIG-bench, a human expert with unlimited time could achieve a perfect score. Thus, our reported ‘‘expert’’ scores are in actuality strong lower bounds to a theoretical maximum of human performance.
