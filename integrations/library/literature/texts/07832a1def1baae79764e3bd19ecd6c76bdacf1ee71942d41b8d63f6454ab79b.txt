Finally, we noticed qualitatively that small language models often did not generate a final answer that could be parsed, due to either repetitions or logic that never arrived at a final answer. In summary, the success of chain-of-thought reasoning as a result of model scale is a complicated phenomena that likely involves a variety of emergent abilities (semantic understanding, symbol mapping, staying on topic, arithmetic ability, faithfulness, etc).
