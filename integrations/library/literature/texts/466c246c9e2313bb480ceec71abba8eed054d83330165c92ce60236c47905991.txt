However, it is not necessarily useful to study psychology, programs, or representations in terms of neurotransmitters, assembly, or neurons, respectively. Representations are worth studying at their own level, and if we reduce them to a lower-level of analysis, we may obscure important complex phenomena. Representation engineering is not applied mechanistic interpretability, just as biology is not applied chemistry. However, there can be overlap. Building only from the bottom up is an inadequate strategy for studying the world. To analyze complex phenomena, we must also look from the top down. We can work to build staircases between the bottom and top level (Gell-Mann, 1995), so we should have research on mechanistic interpretability (bottom-up transparency) and representation reading (top-down transparency).
