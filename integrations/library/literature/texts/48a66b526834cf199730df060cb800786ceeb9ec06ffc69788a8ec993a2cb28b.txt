In other words, are the gains with posthoc calibration (e.g., Platt scaling, isotonic regression, and beta calibration) additive with acute? To test this, we posthoc calibrate the outputs of our acute systems to reduce calibration error (smECE) on APIGen and SCITLDR. Figure˜8 shows performance averaged across the 7 LLMs we tested. We find that when applying posthoc calibration to the base systems (denoted in black), auc-euro generally increases and calibration error (smECE) decreases, especially for SCITLDR. For APIGen on acute cosine, posthoc calibration has negligible impact with isotonic regression actually lowering performance. Per model results are in Tables˜12, 13, 14, 15, 16, 17, 18 and 19.
