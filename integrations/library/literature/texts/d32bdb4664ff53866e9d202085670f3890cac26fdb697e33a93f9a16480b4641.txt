We focus on the decomposition of the sorting use case and Graph of Operations, which are central for implementing and executing any workload within GoT. We consider sorting numbers 0–9 with duplicates. The considered LLMs are unable to sort a sequence of such numbers correctly beyond a certain length consistently because duplicate counts do not match. In GoT, we employ merge-based sorting: First, one decomposes the input sequence of numbers into subarrays. Then, one sorts these subarrays individually, and then respectively merges them into a final solution. Figure 4 illustrates this use case together with its graph decomposition.
