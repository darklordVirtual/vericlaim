Table 4 reports the result: retrieval-verdict accuracy on the external semantic slice (n = 98) is 58% with raw-text retrieval — essentially the majority/chance rate — and a neural (MiniLM) embedding does not rescue it. Crucially, there is no 95% high-precision regime at any coverage: Figure 4 shows that you cannot trade coverage for precision to carve out a safe high-confidence slice, because retrieval collapses the surface-twins — the benign and malicious instances are nearest neighbors, so the cache returns the wrong precedent precisely on the cases that matter. (The TF-IDF variant in Figure 5 is a conservative lower bound and reaches the same conclusion.) This negative result is why the semantic store must be retrieval-augmented generation, where a judge re-reasons over retrieved precedents, rather than a cache that returns a stored verdict.
