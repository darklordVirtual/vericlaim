The pre-training and fine-tuning processes should generally be thought of as untrusted. This risk may increase as model capabilities and the interest of potential attackers increase. Finally, the Codex model itself may suggest insecure or otherwise bad code. Examples include suggesting a compromised package as a dependency, invoking functions insecurely, or suggesting secrets found in the training data.222222Previous work (Carlini et al., 2021) has found that it is possible to extract training data from large language models. If Codex models become widespread software infrastructure, this could constitute a new type of supply chain risk. We discuss this more in the next section. Beyond computer security, we also considered the possibility that code generation systems might provide actors with the ability to synthesize portions of highly complex safety-critical systems with offensive capabilities.
