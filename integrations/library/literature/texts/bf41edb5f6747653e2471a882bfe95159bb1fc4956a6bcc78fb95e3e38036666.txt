The verifiability of claims is also a key theme in the study of trust in the context of international relations and arms control [138]. Ronald Reagan’s famous "trust but verify" (a proverb taught to him by advisor on Russian affairs Suzanne Massie [139]) emphasized the value of generating and assessing evidence of compliance with arms control agreements between the United States and Soviet Union. AI, verification, and arms control are discussed further in Appendix IV. This report is not intended to make novel contributions to the theory of trust or trustworthiness, but rather to explore verifiable claims as a building block of trustworthy AI development. When we use terms such as "earn trust" or "calibrate trust" in the report, these are meant to refer to cases in which evidence is provided to substantiate claims about an actor’s behavior (including AI development).
