In practice, is sampled as a continuous language sequence, and the decomposition of thoughts (e.g. is each a phrase, a sentence, or a paragraph) is left ambiguous. Self-consistency with CoT (CoT-SC) (Wang et al., 2022) is an ensemble approach that samples i.i.d. chains of thought: , then returns the most frequent output: . CoT-SC improves upon CoT, because there are generally different thought processes for the same problem (e.g. different ways to prove the same theorem), and the output decision can be more faithful by exploring a richer set of thoughts. However, within each chain there is no local exploration of different thought steps, and the “most frequent” heuristic only applies when the output space is limited (e.g. multi-choice QA).
