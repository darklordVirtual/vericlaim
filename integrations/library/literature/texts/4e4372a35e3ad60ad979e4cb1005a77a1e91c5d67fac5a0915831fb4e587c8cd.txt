In Figures App.4 and 8 we show how the notion of performance on a task, and the degree to which a task exhibits breakthrough behavior can depend sharply on the metric used to probe performance. Metrics such as accuracy or exact string match often show non-smooth behavior, while metrics such as likelihood or ROUGE can vary smoothly. Nonetheless, the existence of smooth metrics does not preclude breakthrough behavior. In Figure App.4b, though the ROUGE score increases smoothly, the model only learns to copy words from the input and never solves the task. In Figure 8b, though the likelihood of the true target improves smoothly, the likelihood of incorrect targets freeze out at a particular scale.
