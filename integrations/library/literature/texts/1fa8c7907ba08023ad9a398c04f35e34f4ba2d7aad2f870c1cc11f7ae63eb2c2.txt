One important challenge with models which seek to be interpretable or provide explanations for their reasoning is that of faithfulness. While our primary emphasis is not on the explainability benefits that STaR may bring, there is a fundamental challenge around evaluating explanations and rationales: namely, faithfulness [33, 34]. [34] describe faithful explanations as those which “accurately [represent] the
reasoning process behind the model’s prediction.” While STaR encourages the use of reasoning in rationales which leads the model to correct answers, it is difficult, if not impossible, to ensure that the rationales reflect the model’s internal processing. For example, it is straightforward to imagine the model implicitly selecting a particular answer immediately and then generating a rationale to justify that selected answer.
