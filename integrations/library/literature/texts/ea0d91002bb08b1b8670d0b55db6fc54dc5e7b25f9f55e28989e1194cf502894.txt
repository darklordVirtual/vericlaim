While Zero-shot-CoT is conceptually simple, it uses prompting twice to extract both reasoning and answer, as explained in Figure 2. In contrast, the zero-shot baseline (see the bottom-left in Figure 1) already uses prompting in the form of “The answer is”, to extract the answers in correct formats. Few-shot prompting, standard or CoT, avoids needing such answer-extraction prompting by explicitly designing the few-shot example answers to end in such formats (see the top-right and top-left in Figure 1). In summary, Few-shot-CoT (Wei et al., 2022) requires careful human engineering of a few prompt examples with specific answer formats per task, while Zero-shot-CoT requires less engineering but requires prompting LLMs twice. 1st prompt: reasoning extraction
