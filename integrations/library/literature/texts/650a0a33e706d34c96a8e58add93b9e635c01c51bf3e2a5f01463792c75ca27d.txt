Figure 1: Example inputs and outputs of GPT-3 with (a) standard Few-shot ((Brown et al., 2020)), (b) Few-shot-CoT ((Wei et al., 2022)), (c) standard Zero-shot, and (d) ours (Zero-shot-CoT). Similar to Few-shot-CoT, Zero-shot-CoT facilitates multi-step reasoning (blue text) and reach correct answer where standard prompting fails. Unlike Few-shot-CoT using step-by-step reasoning examples per task, ours does not need any examples and just uses the same prompt “Let’s think step by step” across all tasks (arithmetic, symbolic, commonsense, and other logical reasoning tasks). Scaling up the size of language models has been key ingredients of recent revolutions in natural language processing (NLP) (Vaswani et al., 2017; Devlin et al., 2019; Raffel et al., 2020; Brown et al., 2020; Thoppilan et al., 2022; Rae et al., 2021; Chowdhery et al., 2022). The success of large language models (LLMs) is often attributed to (in-context) few-shot or zero-shot learning. It can solve various tasks by simply conditioning the models on a few examples (few-shot) or instructions describing the task (zero-shot).
