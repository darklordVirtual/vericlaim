Large language models perform poorly on BIG-bench relative to expert humans (Figure 1). We also find that model performance on the benchmark increases more slowly with scale than has been seen on other recent benchmarks. While naive extrapolation suggests that solving BIG-bench is infeasible
using
expected near-future compute budgets,
improved techniques have already led to off-trend improvements. Innovations in training (Sanh et al., 2022; Wei et al., 2021; Askell et al., 2021; Hoffmann et al., 2022), prompting (Nye et al., 2021; Wei et al., 2022), and inference strategy (Cobbe et al., 2021), have all shown improved performance relative to scale. Results from Gopher (Rae et al., 2021) and PaLM (Chowdhery et al., 2022) on BIG-bench tasks also suggest that newer models can do dramatically better than predicted by naive log-linear extrapolation from smaller models, even without fundamental changes to architecture or training procedure. Furthermore, task performance sometimes improves suddenly and rapidly beyond a particular model scale (Figure 7b).
