LLM-based agents: they convert natural-language instructions into
executable tool calls without human mediation. LGA preserves this autonomous execution for verified calls;
human review is required only for escalated blocks, not the
normal execution path. Existing defenses target the text-generation layer and do not intercept unauthorized tool invocations arising from semantically benign-looking inputs. Content-safety systems such as Llama Guard (Inan et al., 2023)
and NeMo Guardrails (Rebedea et al., 2023) filter harmful text
generation but lack tool-call–level interception (see Sect. 2 for a survey). Agent frameworks such as LangChain (Chase, 2022) and
AutoGen (Wu et al., 2024) provide tool-chaining interfaces but
lack execution-layer isolation. Benchmarks such as InjecAgent (Zhan et al., 2024) and
AgentDojo (Debenedetti et al., 2024) quantify attack success
rates but do not propose deployable mitigations.
