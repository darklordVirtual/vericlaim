Figure 1 shows the aggregate performance of all models evaluated on BIG-bench. We find that average performance improves both with compute scale and with number of shots (see Figure App.2 for a more detailed look at how individual tasks contribute to aggregate performance). However, even the strongest models perform poorly overall when compared with expert human rater performance. As mentioned in Section 2.1,
each BIG-bench task has a unique preferred metric, as well as high and low values for the task on this metric, as specified by the task authors. The low value indicates poor performance on the task, and the high value indicates excellent performance. When computing aggregate performance plots, we normalize the raw preferred score by subtracting the low score and normalizing the low to high score range to lie within 0--100. Under this normalized preferred metric, tasks are calibrated so that a score of 0 corresponds to poor performance, and a score of 100 corresponds to very good performance. On some tasks, model scores can be less than 0 (e.g., if a model does worse than random guessing on multiple-choice tasks), or greater than 100.
