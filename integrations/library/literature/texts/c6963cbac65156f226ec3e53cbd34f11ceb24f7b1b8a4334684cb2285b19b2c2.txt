Beyond LRD, a broad goal of machine learning is to develop a single model that can be used across a wide range of problems. Models today are typically specialized to solve problems from a particular domain (e.g. images, audio, text, time-series), and enable a narrow range of capabilities (e.g. efficient training, fast generation, handling irregularly sampled data). This specialization is typically expressed via domain-specific preprocessing, inductive biases, and architectures. Sequence models provide a general framework for solving many of these problems with reduced specialization
– e.g. Vision Transformers for image classification with less 2D information [12]. However, most models such as Transformers generally still require substantial specialization per task to achieve high performance. Deep SSMs in particular have conceptual strengths that suggest they may be promising as a general sequence modeling solution.
