For many critical use cases, it is important not just that models are accurate, but also that they do not assign high confidence to wrong answers. In this section, we quantify how well calibrated model uncertainty estimates are on BIG-bench, and how this calibration changes with model scale. Modern neural networks
are often poorly calibrated, showing overconfidence (Nguyen & O’Connor, 2015; Hendrycks & Gimpel, 2017), and calibration often does not improve with increasing depth or width (Guo et al., 2017). Hendrycks et al. (2019) found that pre-training can improve calibration of the model for vision tasks. In the context of large language models, Brown et al. (2020) observed that GPT-3’s predictions are not well calibrated compared to human raters. Desai & Durrett (2020) observed that while pre-trained language models are often calibrated in-domain, their calibration error out-of-domain is much worse. Furthermore, Jiang et al. (2020) studied calibration on generative language models (T5, BART, and GPT-2) and found that their predictive probabilities on question-answering tasks are not well calibrated.
