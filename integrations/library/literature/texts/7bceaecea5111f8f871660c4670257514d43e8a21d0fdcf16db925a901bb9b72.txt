Although beyond the scope of the present work, it will be interesting to study whether they hold more generally. 3.4.2 Breakthrough behavior is sensitive to details of task specification

Breakthrough behavior is consistent with the model suddenly gaining new skills in an abrupt way. Careful analysis of task behavior, however,
suggests that the underlying change in model capabilities is generally more smooth. These smooth improvements can be exposed by decomposing tasks in ways that better capture partial progress. We describe some of those ways in the remainder of this section. Breakthrough behavior is suggestive of ‘‘aha! moments’’ in psychology (Kounios & Beeman, 2009), where humans experience sudden understanding all at once.333
aha! moments occur over time, rather than with greater model scale. On the other hand, increasing model training time and increasing model scale often produce similar performance profiles, for instance in the context of double descent (Nakkiran et al., 2021; Nakkiran, 2019; Nakkiran et al., 2020). It is an interesting question whether aha! moments in humans are similarly associated with smooth underlying improvements in understanding.
