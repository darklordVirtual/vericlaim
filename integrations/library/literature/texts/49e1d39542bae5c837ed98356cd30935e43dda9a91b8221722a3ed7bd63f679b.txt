This suggests that right now increasing investments in alignment of existing language models is more cost-effective than training larger models—at least for our customers’ natural language task distribution. 2. We’ve seen some evidence that InstructGPT generalizes ‘following instructions’ to settings that we don’t supervise it in, for example on non-English language tasks and code-related tasks. This is an important property because it’s prohibitively expensive to have humans supervise models on every task they perform. More research is needed to study how well this generalization scales with increased capabilities; see Christiano et al., (2021) for recent research in this direction. 3.
