This is one key reason why code generated by the Codex models should be treated as untrusted by those using it for research or development until they have reviewed and verified its accuracy and fitness for purpose themselves. As the research community explores more powerful code generation tools that might be increasingly relied on, these issues become even more relevant and holistic assessment across verticals such as bias becomes crucial for determining safety for deployment. In this section, we discuss our probes for bias in three areas: classification completions in sensitive domains; generated text such as comments or docstrings; and package import suggestions. Note that in this appendix, we explore the biases reflected in the ”unfiltered” outputs of Codex models, which in turn were built for research purposes. Thus, these results may not all be representative of a production setting where mitigations such as output filters or alignment techniques may be applied.
