Unless otherwise specified by the use of the adjective ”formal” or other context, this report uses the word verification in a looser sense. Formal verification seeks mathematical proof that a certain technical claim is true with certainty (subject to certain assumptions). In contrast, this report largely focuses on claims that are unlikely to be demonstrated with absolute certainty, but which can be shown to be likely or unlikely to be true through relevant arguments and evidence. as falsifiable statements for which evidence and arguments can be brought to bear on the likelihood of those claims being true. While the degree of attainable certainty will vary across different claims and contexts, we hope to show that greater degrees of evidence can be provided for claims about AI development than is typical today.
