The first feature we'll consider is an Arabic Script feature, A/1/3450. It activates in response to text in Arabic, Farsi, Urdu (and possibly other languages), which use the Arabic script. This feature is quite specific and relatively sensitive to Arabic script, and effectively invisible if we view the model in terms of individual neurons. Activation Specificity

Our first step is to show that this feature fires almost exclusively on text in Arabic script. We give each token an "Arabic script" score using an estimated likelihood ratio \log(P(s|\text{Arabic Script}) / P(s)), and break down the histogram of feature activations by that score. Arabic text is quite rare in our overall data distribution – just 0.13% of training tokens — but it makes up 81% of the tokens on which our feature is active. That percentage varies significantly by feature activity level, from 25% when the feature is barely active to 98% when the feature activation is above 5. We also show dataset examples demonstrating different levels of feature activity. In interpreting them, it's important to note that Arabic Unicode characters are often split into multiple tokens.
