Table 1: A leveled, matrixed approach toward classifying systems on the path to AGI based on depth (performance) and breadth (generality) of capabilities. The assignment of example systems to cells is approximate. Unambiguous classification of AI systems will require a standardized benchmark of tasks, as we discuss in Section 5. Note that general systems that broadly perform at a level N may be able to perform a narrow subset of tasks at higher levels. The “Competent AGI” level, which has not been achieved by any public systems at the time of writing, best corresponds to many prior conceptions of AGI, and may precipitate rapid societal change once achieved.
