IO-Aware Runtime Optimization. The broad concept of optimizing for reading and writing to fast/slow memory has a long history in computer science and has been known by many names. We draw the most direct connection to the literature of analyzing I/O complexity in this work [1], but concepts of memory hierarchies are fundamental and has appeared in many forms, from the working set model [21], to data locality [86], to the Roofline model of arithmetic intensity [85], to analyses of scalability [59], to standard textbook treatments of computer architecture [40].
