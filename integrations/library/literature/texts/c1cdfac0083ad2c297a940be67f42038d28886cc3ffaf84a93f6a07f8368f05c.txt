However, we believe the apparent conflict can be explained by the difference in the scale of the supervision. The data scaling trend in Figure 4(a) suggests that a small amount of process supervision and a large amount of outcome supervision do in fact lead to similar performance, consistent with the results from Uesato et al. We believe these results make a strong case for using process supervision.
