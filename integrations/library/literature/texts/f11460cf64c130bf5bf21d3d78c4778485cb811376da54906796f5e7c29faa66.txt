Appropriately chosen prompting, or in-context examples, can alter output bias. In Figure 13 we show that indeed, for bbq_lite, in the case of ambiguous prompting, few-shot prompting indicating neutrality as the desired response significantly reduces model bias. These results seem consistent with work such as Askell et al. (2021), where the authors use a hand-designed prompt as a stronger baseline for helpfulness, harmlessness, and honesty. Note that these observations seem qualitatively similar across all the categories of social bias probed in BIG-bench. See Figure 12 and Figure App.3.
