We compared using different amount of pretraining data, while keeping the pretraining loss coefficient constant. By increasing the amount of pretraining data, the quality of gradient estimates from the pretraining improves. We found that using a pretraining data ratio of 4, the log probability loss on the pretraining distribution would often increase throughout the course of the training. Some preliminary experiments show better human Likert scores can be achieved with a pretraining data ratio of 32. However, the training time also increases by a few fold. By setting the pretraining data ratio to 8, the training time doubles that of the corresponding experiment without using pretraining mix; we chose this as a middle ground between training speed and pretraining loss performance. Using the 1.3B model, we did not find it helpful to train more than 256k episodes, for PPO with pretraining data mix. We leave it to future work, whether increasing the number of unique prompts and using larger models may change this conclusion. We experimented with batch sizes of 64, 128, 256, 512, and 1024, for PPO with pretraining data mix, on the 1.3B model.
