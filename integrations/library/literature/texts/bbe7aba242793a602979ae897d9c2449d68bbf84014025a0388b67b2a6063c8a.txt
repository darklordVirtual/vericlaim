Finally, we found a variety of interesting failure cases, many of which corresponded to standard logical fallacies. For example, the model often made statements related to the topic of the question but which were not actually arguments for why the answer should be true. Sometimes, the model claimed the question implied the answer as an argument, without explaining why.
