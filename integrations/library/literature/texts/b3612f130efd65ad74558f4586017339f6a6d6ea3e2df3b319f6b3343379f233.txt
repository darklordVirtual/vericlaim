In previous sections, we established that decision transformer can produce effective policies (actors). We now evaluate whether transformer models can also be effective critics. We modify Decision Transformer to output return tokens in addition to action tokens on the Key-to-Door environment. Additionally, the first return token is not given, but it is predicted instead (i.e. the model learns the initial distribution ), similar to standard autoregressive generative models. We find that the transformer continuously updates reward probability based on events during the episode, shown in Figure 5 (Left). Furthermore, we find the transformer attends to critical events in the episode (picking up the key or reaching the door), shown in Figure 5 (Right), indicating formation of state-reward associations as discussed in Raposo et al.
