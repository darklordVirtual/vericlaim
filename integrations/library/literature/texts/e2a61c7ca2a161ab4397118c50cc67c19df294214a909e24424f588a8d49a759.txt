When scored using a multiple-choice metric, however, the performance improvements appear much more gradual (see Figure 9b). When manually examining model output, we see that models start producing movie titles after some scale, begin identifying semantics of the emojis at even larger scales, and in some cases produce the correct answer at the largest scales. A representative example is shown in Figure 9c. Despite these elements of smooth behavior, subthreshold improvement need not explain full task performance. Though in many cases we can identify smoothly improving metrics or subtasks for tasks that otherwise see no model improvement, or breakthrough behavior, it is not always clear what these smooth proxies teach us about performance on the true task. As a concrete example, perfect mastery of identifying legal chess moves need not translate into strong performance on checkmate_in_one.
