Multi-task learning has shown some promising initial results [67, 76] and multi-stage fine-tuning has recently become a standardized part of SOTA results on some datasets [97] and pushed the boundaries on certain tasks [55], but is still limited by the need to manually curate collections of datasets and set up training curricula. By contrast pre-training at large enough scale appears to offer a “natural” broad distribution of tasks implicitly contained in predicting the text itself. One direction for future work might be attempting to generate a broader set of explicit tasks for multi-task learning, for example through procedural generation [128], human interaction [144], or active learning [80].
