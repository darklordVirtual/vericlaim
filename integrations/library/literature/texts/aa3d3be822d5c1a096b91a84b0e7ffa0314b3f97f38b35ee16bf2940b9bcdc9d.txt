The results indicate that the performance is improved if the text is written in a way that encourages chain of thought reasoning, i.e., the templates are within "instructive" category. However, the difference in accuracy is significant depending on the sentence. In this experiment, "Let’s think step by step." achieves the best results. Interestingly, it is found that different templates encourage the model to express reasoning quite differently (see Appendix B for sample outputs by each template). In contrast, when we use misleading or irrelevant templates, the performance does not improve. It remains an open question how to automatically create better templates for Zero-shot-CoT.
