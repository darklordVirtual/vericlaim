Companies can themselves adopt incident reporting systems to raise user and public awareness of a system’s use in consequential scenarios. Similar to the “How Am I Driving?” sticker on vehicles for employee driver feedback (Strahilevitz, 2006), such prompts could nudge harm reporting for impacted stakeholders. These notices, however, can easily become overwhelming. Some have noted, for example, that the privacy disclosure pop-ups implemented in response to the EU’s ePrivacy Directive have increased “consent fatigue” among users without meaningfully improving user privacy (Sanchez-Rola et al., 2019; Trevisan et al., 2019). In some cases, users were purposefully inundated with notices designed to induce them to consent by default (Soe et al., 2020; Nouwens et al., 2020; Matte et al., 2020; Miyazaki, 2008). These types of informational disclosures can also distract regulators from more pressing problems.
