Figure 3: A case study of FrugalGPT on the HEADLINES dataset. (a) The cascade strategy that FrugalGPT learned on this dataset with overall budget $6.5, one fifth of GPT-4’s cost. FrugalGPT avoids querying GPT-4 as long as GPT-J and J1-L produce high-quality answers. (b) Sometimes GPT-4 makes a mistake, but FrugalGPT learns to use the correct answers by J-1 and GPT-J. (c) Overall, we observe that FrugalGPT reduces the cost by 80%, while improves the accuracy by 1.5% compared to GPT-4. Let us begin with a case study on the HEADLINES dataset. We set the budget to be $6.5, which is one-fifth of GPT-4’s cost. We employ a DistilBERT [SDCW19] tailored to regression as the scoring function. It is important to note that DistilBERT is considerably smaller and therefore less expensive than all LLMs considered here. As depicted in Figure 3 (a), the learned FrugalGPT sequentially calls GPT-J, J1-L, and GPT-4. For any given query, it first extracts an answer from GPT-J. If the score of this answer is greater than 0.96, the answer is accepted as the final response. Otherwise, J1-L is queried.
