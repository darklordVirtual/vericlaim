3. We were able to mitigate most of the performance degradations introduced by our fine-tuning. If this was not the case, these performance degradations would constitute an alignment tax—an additional cost for aligning the model. Any technique with a high tax might not see adoption. To avoid incentives for future highly capable AI systems to remain unaligned with human intent, there is a need for alignment techniques that have low alignment tax. To this end, our results are good news for RLHF as a low-tax alignment technique. 4. We’ve validated alignment techniques from research in the real world. Alignment research has historically been rather abstract, focusing on either theoretical results (Soares et al.,, 2015), small synthetic domains (Christiano et al.,, 2018; Leike et al.,, 2017), or training ML models on public NLP datasets (Ziegler et al.,, 2019; Stiennon et al.,, 2020).
