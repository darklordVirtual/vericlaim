However, the underlying mechanics and efficacy of self-correction in LLMs remain underexplored. A fundamental question arises: If an LLM possesses the ability to self-correct, why doesn’t it simply offer the correct answer in its initial attempt? This paper delves deeply into this paradox, critically examining the self-correction capabilities of LLMs, with a particular emphasis on reasoning (Wei et al., 2022; Zhou et al., 2023b; Huang & Chang, 2023). To study this, we first define the concept of intrinsic self-correction, a scenario wherein the model endeavors to rectify its initial responses based solely on its inherent capabilities, without the crutch of external feedback. Such a setting is crucial because high-quality external feedback is often unavailable in many real-world applications. Moreover, it is vital to understand the intrinsic capabilities of LLMs.
