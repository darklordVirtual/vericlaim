Yamins et al. (2014) went as far as to title their work in the spirit that performance over such tasks implies brain alignment. Antonello & Huth (2024) posited that it is less the particular task and more the generality of the representations that explain their alignment with biological representations. Further, Conwell et al. (2022) showed that training data plays a large role in alignment. Psychophysical studies have also shown agreement between how humans perceive visual similarity and how models do, even when the models are trained on tasks, such as self-supervised prediction, that are seemingly unrelated to mimicking human perception (Zhang et al., 2018).
