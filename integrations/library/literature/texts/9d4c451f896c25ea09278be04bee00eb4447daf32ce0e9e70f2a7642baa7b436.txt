To better understand the behavior of Zero-shot-CoT, we manually investigated randomly selected examples generated by Instruct-GPT3 with Zero-shot-CoT prompting. See Appendix C for examples, where some of the observations include: (1) In commonsense reasoning (CommonsenseQA), Zero-shot-CoT often produces flexible and reasonable chain of thought even when the final prediction is not correct. Zero-shot-CoT often output multiple answer choices when the model find it is difficult to narrow it down to one (see Table 3 for examples). (2) In arithmetic reasoning (MultiArith), Zero-shot-CoT and Few-shot-CoT show substantial differences regarding the error patterns. First, Zero-shot-CoT tends to output unnecessary steps of reasoning after getting the correct prediction, which results in changing the prediction to incorrect one.
