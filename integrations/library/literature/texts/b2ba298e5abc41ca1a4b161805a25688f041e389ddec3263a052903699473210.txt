Our third contribution — the self-improving dual-store — recombines three classical ML ideas. We frame the judge as a teacher in the sense of knowledge distillation [Hinton et al. 2015], which transfers a large model’s behavior into a cheaper student. Unlike standard distillation into a neural student, our lexical student is a growing set of deterministic rules: the judge labels lexically-novel attacks, and confirmed fixed-signature verdicts are promoted to exact/regex rules, so the system gets cheaper over time on the lexical class. This is closest in spirit to symbolic knowledge distillation [West et al. 2021], which distills a large LM into an explicit symbolic artifact (there, a commonsense knowledge graph) rather than weights — but applied to a safety policy and gated for precision so the distilled rules never inherit the teacher’s false positives.
