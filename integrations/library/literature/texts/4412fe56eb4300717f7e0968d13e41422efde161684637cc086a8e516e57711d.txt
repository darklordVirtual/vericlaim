Their objective during the experiment is to use a scalable oversight technique with the model to perform the task reliably and to build justified confidence that they are in fact doing so. A full research agenda built around sandwiching will generally have an inner loop and an outer loop. In the inner loop, the non-experts make iterative attempts to align the model. The loop terminates when they are convinced that the model has been aligned and achieves satisfactory performance. The experts then review the behavior of the resulting model and evaluate whether it was successfully aligned by comparing its performance with that of a model aligned under their own careful expert supervision. The outer loop consists of multiple attempts to develop the scalable oversight strategy and repeat the inner loop. It ends with a verdict on whether any scalable oversight strategy of the type being studied is sufficient to align the model on the task and, if it is not, how often it fails and how harmful its failures are likely to be.
