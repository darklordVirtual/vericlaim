It can be tempting to stop evaluations after plotting the coverage and set size, but certain important questions remain unanswered. A good spread of set sizes is generally better, but it does not necessarily indicate that the sets adapt properly to the difficulty of . Above seeing that the set sizes have dynamic range, we will need to verify that large sets occur for hard examples. We next formalize this notion and give metrics for evaluating it.
