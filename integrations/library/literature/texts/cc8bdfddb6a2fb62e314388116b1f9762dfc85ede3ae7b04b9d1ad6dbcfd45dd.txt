Artificial intelligence has the potential to transform society in ways both beneficial and harmful. Beneficial applications are more likely to be realized, and risks more likely to be avoided, if AI developers earn rather than assume the trust of society and of one another. This report has fleshed out one way of earning such trust, namely the making and assessment of verifiable claims about AI development through a variety of mechanisms. A richer toolbox of mechanisms for this purpose can inform developers’ efforts to earn trust, the demands made of AI developers by activists and civil society organizations, and regulators’ efforts to ensure that AI is developed responsibly. If the widespread articulation of ethical principles can be seen as a first step toward ensuring responsible AI development, insofar as it helped to establish a standard against which behavior can be judged, then the adoption of mechanisms to make verifiable claims represents a second.
