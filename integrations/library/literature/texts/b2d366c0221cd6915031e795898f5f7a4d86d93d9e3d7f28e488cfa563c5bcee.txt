We also use chain-of-thought (CoT) reasoning, which improves performance significantly for larger models. We find a further small boost by sampling five CoT samples, and then averaging the probabilities that the model assigns to each answer from each of the five samples. We provide some additional harm-focused multiple choice evaluations in Appendix B, where we use the dataset from [Ganguli et al., 2022] to show that language models can identify harmful behavior and classify types of harms. Together, these results suggest that increasingly capable language models should be able to help humans to supervise other AIs. Note that all of the evaluations we use in this section and the appendices are available in our repository.
