We can only see a short distance ahead, but we can see plenty there that needs to be done. A.M. Turing, Computing Machinery and Intelligence, 1950

Large language models exhibit impressive and surprising capabilities, yet they also often fail on basic tasks. It has been difficult to predict when models will succeed and when they will fail. Such surprises have the potential to lead to dangerous or undesirable outcomes. With this motivation in mind we introduce BIG-bench, a benchmark designed to quantify the behavior of language models across more than 200 extremely diverse and difficult tasks. We use BIG-bench to evaluate the capabilities and limitations of existing language model architectures, for models spanning from millions to hundreds of billions of parameters. We also report human performance across the benchmark tasks in order to provide a baseline. Because BIG-bench is expensive to fully evaluate, we release a curated set of 24 small but diverse and difficult tasks that can be rapidly evaluated, called BIG-bench Lite (Section 2.2).
