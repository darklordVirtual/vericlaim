Decoupled Distribution of Visual and Reasoning Confidence

To further verify that visual and reasoning confidence measure different things, we visualize their distribution across all samples in a 2D heatmap (Figure 6). The results show that the two scores are clearly separated. For instance, when visual confidence is high (the bin), reasoning confidence still varies widely from 0.1 to 1.0. Similarly, even when reasoning confidence is at its highest (), visual confidence ranges from 0.3 to 1.0. This indicates that the model can be certain about what it sees but unsure about its logic, or vice versa.
