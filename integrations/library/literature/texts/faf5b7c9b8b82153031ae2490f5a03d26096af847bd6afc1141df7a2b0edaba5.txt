on a version of our one-layer model with random weights.We generate a model with random weights by randomly shuffling the entries of each weight matrix of the trained transformer used in Run A. This guarantees that the distributions of individual weights match, and differences are due to structure. The resulting features are here, and contain many single-token features (such as "span", "file", ".", and "nature") and some other features firing on seemingly arbitrary subsets of different broadly recognizable contexts (such as LaTeX or code). However, we are unable to construct interpretations for the non-single-token features that make much sense and invite the reader to examine feature visualizations from the model with randomized weights to confirm this for themselves. We conclude that the learning process for the model creates a richer structure in its activations than the distribution of tokens in the dataset alone.
