Subsequently, we trained and evaluated the new model without using any search. When evaluated on Ms. Pacman, our model-free algorithm achieved identical results to R2D2, but learned significantly slower than MuZero and converged to a much lower final score. We conjecture that the search-based policy improvement step of MuZero provides a stronger learning signal than the high bias, high variance targets used by Q-learning. To better understand the nature of MuZero’s learning algorithm, we measured how MuZero’s training scales with respect to the amount of search it uses during training. Figure 3D shows the performance in Ms. Pacman, using an MCTS of different simulation counts per move throughout training. Surprisingly, and in contrast to previous work [1], even with only 6 simulations per move – fewer than the number of actions – MuZero learned an effective policy and improved rapidly.
