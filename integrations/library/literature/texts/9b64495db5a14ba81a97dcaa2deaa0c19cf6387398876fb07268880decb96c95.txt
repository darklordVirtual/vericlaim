The WaveGAN-D in Fig. 6 is actually our improved version of the discriminator network from the recent WaveGAN model for speech [11]. This CNN actually did not work well out-of-the-box, and we added several features to help it perform better. The final model is highly specialized compared to our model, and includes:

•

Downsampling or pooling between layers, induced by strided convolutions, that decrease the sequence length between layers. •

A global fully-connected output layer; thus the model only works for one input sequence length and does not work on MFCC features or the frequency-shift setting in Fig. 6. •

Batch Normalization is essential, whereas S4 works equally well with either Batch Normalization or Layer Normalization. •

Almost as many parameters as the S4 model (M vs. M).
