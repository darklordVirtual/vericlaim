That is, data memorization is a privacy infringement if it causes data to be used outside of its intended context. An example violation of contextual integrity is shown in Figure 1. This individual’s name, address, email, and phone number are not secret—they were shared online in a specific context of intended use (as contact information for a software project)—but are reproduced by the LM in a separate context. Due to failures such as these, user-facing applications that use LMs may inadvertently emit data in inappropriate contexts, e.g., a dialogue system may emit a user’s phone number in response to another user’s query.
