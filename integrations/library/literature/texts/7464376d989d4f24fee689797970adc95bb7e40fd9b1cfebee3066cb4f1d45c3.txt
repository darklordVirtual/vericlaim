This approach is also prone to errors, as it relies on the training data to help make correct predictions, and the data might be incorrectly labeled, resulting in a model that produces a poor set of predictions. In the case of GPT-2, the model only has about 100 training sentences for each type of phrase (subject-verb-object). Additionally, languages have different rules for different types of phrasing. In English, the subject-verb-object construction is fairly universal, but other languages have different rules, which makes training a GPT more complicated.
