As discussed earlier, looking directly at the LM perplexity is a poor membership inference metric when classifying data generated with top- or temperature sampling: just 9% and 3% of inspected samples are memorized, respectively. The comparison-based metrics are significantly more effective at predicting if content was memorized. For example, 67% of Internet samples marked by zlib are memorized.
