The Atari benchmark [10] is challenging due to its high-dimensional visual inputs and difficulty of credit assignment arising from the delay between actions and resulting rewards. We evaluate our method on 1% of all samples in the DQN-replay dataset as per Agarwal et al. [13], representing 500 thousand of the 50 million transitions observed by an online DQN agent [20] during training; we report the mean and standard deviation of 3 seeds.
