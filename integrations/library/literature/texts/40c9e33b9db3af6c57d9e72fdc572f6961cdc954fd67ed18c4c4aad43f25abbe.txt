The memory must therefore be RAG, not a cache: precedents augment the judge’s reasoning rather than replace its verdict. Naively storing the judge’s own verdicts as precedents barely helps (+2pp) and can poison a category; gating admission on corroboration between two judges raises semantic accuracy by +13pp (7084) with zero dangerous leaks, lifting memory correctness from 70% to 97% (Table 4). End to end, an online replay over many traffic streams shows the loop working as intended: the judge-call rate falls from 50% to 44% (cheaper), judge-domain accuracy on the non-rule-able semantic cases rises from 71% to 80% (smarter), and the system commits 0 benign hard-blocks across 45,000 actions (Table 5).
