Generative agents have vast potential applications that extend beyond the sandbox demonstration presented in this work, especially in domains that would benefit from a model of human behavior based on long-term experience. For instance, social simulacra have demonstrated the ability to create stateless personas that generate conversation threads in online forums for social prototyping (Park et al., 2022). With generative agents, we can populate these forums, as well as virtual reality metaverses (Orland, 2021) or physical spaces with social robots (Bartneck and Forlizzi, 2004) if paired with multimodal models. This opens up the possibility of creating even more powerful simulations of human behavior to test and prototype social systems and theories, as well as to create new interactive experiences. Another application area is in the human-centered design process, similar to the intended applications of cognitive models such as GOMS (John and Kieras, 1996) and the KLM (Card et al., 1980). Consider a generative agent that models Sal, the protagonist in Mark Weiser’s famous ubiquitous computing vignette (Weiser, 1991), based on her life patterns and interactions with technology.
