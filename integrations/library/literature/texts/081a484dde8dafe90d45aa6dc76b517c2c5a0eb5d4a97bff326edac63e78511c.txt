Precision, recall, sensitivity, and specificity deal with the confusion matrix directly. The area under the precision recall curve (AUPRC) and the area under the receiver operating characteristic curve (AUROC) both involve summarizing the confusion matrix trading off these quantities. In general, AUROC treats both classes as equal, so it is invariant to risk level. AUPRC is not symmetric and is often used when there are imbalanced classes. In a high (or low) risk scenario the rewards for the classes are imbalanced and thus AUPRC could be used. However, both AUROC and AUPRC are reward-blind, so a MBR type of formulation needs to be used. euro balances calibration and decision-making utility, while being able to appropriately score a confidence estimator across risk levels, taking into account calibration.
