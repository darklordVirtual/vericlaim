(2024) show well-trained vision encoders on large datasets exhibit high semantic similarity with language encoders regardless of the training paradigm (supervised, self-supervised, or language-supervised). Sharma et al. (2024) probed the visual knowledge of LLMs trained only on language data, by converting images into code that an LLM can process. They found that LLMs have rich knowledge of visual structures, to the extent that decent visual representations can be trained on images generated solely by querying an LLM to produce code and rendering the response. In visual generation, LLMs show abilities to augment captions with visual structures (e.g., bounding boxes) and improve generation quality (Betker et al., 2023; Lian et al., 2023a, b; Wu et al., 2023). Over other modalities, Ngo & Kim (2024) showed auditory models are also roughly aligned with LLMs up to a linear transformation, and Ng et al. (2023) demonstrated the effectiveness of using pre-trained LLMs for facial motion prediction.
