We can use to provide either outcome or process supervision for smaller models. We determine the labels for individual steps based on the step-level probabilities outputted by . To do this, we set an arbitrary threshold: any step that assigns a negative label with greater than 20% probability is considered incorrect. We choose this threshold based on the observation that is slightly miscalibrated in the direction of favoring positive labels. To provide process supervision for a solution, we directly return the step-level labels (positive or negative) provided by , up until the first step that is marked as negative. This mimics our true human data collection process. To provide outcome supervision, we mark the solution as correct if and only if considers every step to be correct (using the same thresholding logic).
