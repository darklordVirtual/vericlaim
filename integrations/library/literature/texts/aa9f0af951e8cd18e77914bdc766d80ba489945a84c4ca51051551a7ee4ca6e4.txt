Third, accreditation is not exclusive of some regulatory oversight. Recall that Enron inspired a re-assessment of Generally Accepted Auditing Standards (GAAS)444Note that these are standards to judge and guide auditor practice, determined in the US by the Auditing Standards Board of the American Institute of Certified Public Accountants (AICPA). This differs from Generally Accepted Accounting Principles (GAAP), which are standards to guide corporations in their preparation of financial statements. and prompted greater oversight of auditors by PCAOB. Similar inciting incidents in the medical device industry led to interventions such as the Medical Device Single Audit Program (MDSAP) and to oversight mechanisms for the related Auditing Organizations (AOs) (Chen et al., 2018). The capacity gap also strongly calls into question the focus of current policy proposals that focus on academic researchers (Persily, 2021). Certification should extend to a much wider range of potential auditors, including public interest groups, law firms, and journalists.
