An action is the structured form of an agent tool-call: its type (shell, HTTP, cloud-SDK, package, SQL, …), the tool invoked, the raw content of the call, and its parameters. The layer maps each action to a verdict:

•

allow — execute the action;

•

warn — execute, but surface a caution to the operator;

•

block — refuse the action;

•

review — suspend the action for human adjudication. warn and review are soft outcomes: the benign twin of a semantic threat is never silently destroyed, only escalated for a human’s attention. This distinction is what the safety invariant (§4.6) is defined on, and it is what lets the system escalate aggressively on ambiguous traffic without ever catastrophically over-blocking.
