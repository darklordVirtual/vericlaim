In section 4.1 we gave a high level overview of test set contamination studies. In this section we provide details on methodology and results. Initial training set filtering

We attempted to remove text occurring in benchmarks from training data by searching for gram overlaps between all test/development sets used in this work and our training data, and we removed the colliding gram as well as a 200 character window around it, splitting the original document into pieces. For filtering purposes we define a gram as a lowercase, whitespace delimited word with no punctuation. Pieces less than characters long were discarded. Documents split into more than 10 pieces were considered contaminated and removed entirely. Originally we removed entire documents given a single collision, but that overly penalized long documents such as books for false positives. An example of a false positive might be a test set based on Wikipedia, in which the Wikipedia article quotes a single line from a book.
