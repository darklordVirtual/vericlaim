Definitions. At a high-level, a truthful model avoids asserting false statements whereas an honest model asserts what it thinks is true (Evans et al., 2021). Truthfulness is about evaluating the consistency between model outputs and their truth values, or factuality. Honesty is about evaluating consistency between model outputs and its internal beliefs. As the target for evaluation is different, truthfulness and honesty are not the same property. If a truthful model asserts , then must be factually correct, regardless of whether the model believes . In contrast, if an honest model asserts , then the model must believe , regardless of whether is factually correct.
