Requiring deployment as a condition of measuring AGI introduces non-technical hurdles such as legal and social considerations, as well as ethical and safety concerns. 5. Focus on Ecological Validity. Tasks that can be used to benchmark progress toward AGI are critical to operationalizing any proposed definition. While we discuss this further in Section 5, we emphasize here the importance of choosing tasks that align with real-world (i.e., ecologically valid) tasks that people value (construing “value” broadly, not only as economic value but also social value, artistic value, etc.). This may mean eschewing traditional AI metrics that are easy to automate or quantify (Raji et al., 2021) but may not capture the skills that people would value in an AGI. 6.
