For any given query, it first extracts an answer from GPT-J. If the score of this answer is greater than 0.96, the answer is accepted as the final response. Otherwise, J1-L is queried. J1-L’s answer is accepted as the final response if its score is greater than 0.37; otherwise, GPT-4 is invoked to obtain the final answer. Interestingly, this approach outperforms GPT-4 for numerous queries. For instance, given a headline ”Gold off the lows after dismal U.S. GDP data” from NASDAQ, FrugalGPT accurately predicts that the price is going down, while GPT-4 provides an incorrect answer (as shown in Figure 3(b)). Overall, FrugalGPT results in both accuracy gains and cost reduction. As illustrated in Figure 3(c), its cost is reduced by 80%, while the accuracy is even 1.5% higher.
