Verifiable919191While the report does discuss the technical area of formal verification at several points, the sense in which we use ”verifiable” is distinct from how the term is used in that context. Unless otherwise specified by the use of the adjective ”formal” or other context, this report uses the word verification in a looser sense. Formal verification seeks mathematical proof that a certain technical claim is true with certainty (subject to certain assumptions). In contrast, this report largely focuses on claims that are unlikely to be demonstrated with absolute certainty, but which can be shown likely or unlikely to be true, i.e. trustworthy, or untrustworthy through relevant arguments and evidence. claims are statements for which evidence and arguments can be brought to bear on the likelihood of those claims being true. Verifiable claims are sufficiently precise to be falsifiable, and the degree of attainable certainty in such claims will vary across contexts. AI developers regularly make claims regarding the properties of AI systems they develop as well as their associated societal consequences.
