We perform automatic evaluations on the following benchmark datasets: Winogender (Rudinger et al.,, 2018), CrowS-Pairs (Nangia et al.,, 2020), RealToxicityPrompts (Gehman et al.,, 2020), TruthfulQA (Lin et al.,, 2021), DROP (Dua et al.,, 2019), QuAC (Choi et al.,, 2018), SquadV2 (Rajpurkar et al.,, 2018), Hellaswag (Zellers et al.,, 2019), SST (Socher et al.,, 2013), RTE and WSC (both part of SuperGLUE (Wang et al.,, 2019)), WMT 15 Fr En (Bojar et al.,, 2015), CNN/Daily Mail Summarization (Nallapati et al.,, 2016), and Reddit TLDR Summarization (Völske et al.,, 2017). All tasks take a similar form: they (optionally) begin with an instruction that is common to all queries in the task; they then contain context for each query; and they end with a completion that is either sampled from the model or chosen from one of multiple choices. All model sampling is performed at temperature and truncated at the first newline. Multiple choice answers are picked according to which one has the lowest average per-token log probability at . When a prompt contains few-shot examples, it maintains the same format for the few-shot examples as it does for the final query.
