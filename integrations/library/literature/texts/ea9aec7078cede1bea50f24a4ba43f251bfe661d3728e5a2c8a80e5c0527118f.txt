The x-axis is the language model performance measured over 4M tokens from the OpenWebText dataset (Gokaslan & Cohen, 2019) (see Appendix B for plots with model names). We measure performance using , where bits-per-byte normalizes the cross-entropy by the total bytes in the input text string. The results show a linear relationship between language-vision alignment and language modeling score, where a general trend is that more capable language models align better with more capable vision models. We find that CLIP models, which are trained with explicit language supervision, exhibit a higher level of alignment. However, this alignment decreases after being fine-tuned on ImageNet classification (labeled CLIP (I12K ft)).
