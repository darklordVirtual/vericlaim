Neuroticism and trait anxiety are the most consistent individual-difference correlates of LLM over-reliance and dependency, operating through an affect-regulation and cognitive-offloading pathway. Second, Agreeableness is associated with more positive AI attitudes and greater susceptibility to AI-generated persuasion, especially under anthropomorphic design, though not straightforwardly with problematic dependence. Third, Openness predicts AI adoption, positive attitudes, and trust, but we found no direct evidence that it elevates over-reliance.Conscientiousness shows context-dependent and sometimes opposing effects, while Extraversion exhibits only modest effects on initial trust. Across studies, dispositional propensity-to-trust and system features—chiefly the form of explanation and uncertainty communication—are more proximal predictors of reliance than broad personality traits.
