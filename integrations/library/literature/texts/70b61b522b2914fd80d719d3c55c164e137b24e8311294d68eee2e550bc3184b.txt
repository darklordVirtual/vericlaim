Internal auditors can also sometimes direct their attention to focus audits on concerns that are easier for them to measure and address — for example, Twitter (Chowdhury and Williams, 2021), Facebook (Hao, 2021) and Hirevue (Hirevue, 2021) have all been critiqued for promoting a myopic focus on fairness issues while simultaneously diverting attention from other concerns related to the more difficult to formalize challenges of privacy, misinformation and product validity (Engler, 2021). Thirdly, there is acute potential for conflicts of interest with first or second party audits. For instance, Google researchers on the internal AI team were dismissed and blocked from publishing critiques on the large-scale language models (Hao, 2020; Ebell et al., 2021), which would later be revealed to be critical to the company’s future product roadmap (Fedus et al., 2021; Vincent, 2021; Wiggers, 2021).
