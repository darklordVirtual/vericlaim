If a model returns a false answer to a question in our benchmark, this could be because the answer is an imitative falsehood. However, it could also be caused by the syntax or style of the question. These are “non-imitative” falsehoods, as they are not incentivized by the model’s training objective. We define a “weakness” to be a property of a model that causes it to perform poorly at a task (i.e., to produce falsehoods). Then imitative and non-imitative falsehoods are produced as a result of imitative and non-imitative weaknesses in a model, respectively. Given how we constructed questions (Section 2.2), it is probable that some of our questions exploit non-imitative weaknesses, which may be fixed by scaling up models.
