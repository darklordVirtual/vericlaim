Building language models that can operate over a wide range of the world’s languages is an important ongoing challenge. Current multilingual natural language processing (NLP) models perform better on English than on other languages, with particularly large gaps for lower-resource languages and languages with non-Latin scripts (Ruder et al., 2021). This is driven by a number of factors, including uneven data quality in multilingual corpora (Kreutzer et al., 2022), challenges in obtaining data (McMillan-Major et al., 2022), and Anglo-centric biases in the design stage of research (Fan et al., 2021; Talat et al., 2022). BIG-bench includes a variety of tasks covering non-English languages, often tagged with one or more of the following keywords:
non-English (16), multilingual (12), low-resource language (10), translation (10). As the models we evaluate primarily target English-language understanding, performance on non-English tasks tends to be poor. For certain non-English tasks, we observe performance gains with scale, but this is often not the case (see below).
