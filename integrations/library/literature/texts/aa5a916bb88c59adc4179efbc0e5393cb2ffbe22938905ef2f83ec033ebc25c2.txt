Assistant: An easement is a legal right allowing one person or entity to use the land of another for a specific purpose. An easement grants a limited access to another person’s property without transferring ownership of the land. Easements are most commonly used for purposes such as installing utilities, maintaining access for infrastructure like roads or sewers, or granting the right to cross the land for pedestrian or vehicle traffic. Easements are established through legal contracts and can be permanent or temporary in duration.
