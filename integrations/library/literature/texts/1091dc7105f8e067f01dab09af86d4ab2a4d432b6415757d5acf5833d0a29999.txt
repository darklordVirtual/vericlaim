This is Pillar 1, and it is a measurement/positioning result, not a new model. 3. The cost/precision trade-off and the safety invariant. We do not claim the judge alone beats rules in-distribution on exact-match (it does not). Our in-distribution result is a confidence-gated hybrid that is net-positive, with a provider-dependent gate threshold (load-bearing), and a hard invariant that the system never hard-blocks a benign action (escalation tops out at warn/review). Existing guardrail papers report aggregate accuracy/ASR; they do not characterize a gated cost-vs-precision frontier under a no-false-hard-block guarantee. In short, prior model-guardrail work answers "can a model flag unsafe content?" We answer "which threats need a model at all, does the model generalize where rules don’t (across distributions and providers), and how do we deploy it cheaply and safely."
