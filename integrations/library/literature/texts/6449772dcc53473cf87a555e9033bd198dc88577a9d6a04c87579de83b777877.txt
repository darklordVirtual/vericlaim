SmECE smooths out the histogram using Nadaraya-Watson kernel regression and measures calibration more precisely than ECE, but suffers from the same major drawbacks that ECE does. We introduce a metric that balances calibration with informativeness called expected utility renormalized by the oracle (euro) to assess the decision-making utility of a confidence estimator. euro has just one parameter: the normalized net utility of correctly abstaining from trusting an incorrect generation ().
