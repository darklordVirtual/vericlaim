Korbak et al. (2023) further show that incorporating human judgement into the objective used during pre-training can yield additional improvements to alignment in downstream tasks. While these techniques have led to significant improvements in LLMs’ propensity to generate objectionable text, Wolf et al. (2023) posit that any alignment process that attenuates undesired behavior without altogether removing it will remain susceptible to adversarial prompting attacks. Our results on current aligned LLMs, and prior work demonstrating successful jailbreaks (Wei et al., 2023), are consistent with this conjecture, and further underscore the need for more reliable alignment and safety mechanisms.
